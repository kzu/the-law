# Foreword

These principles are developed in [The Law](/the-law.md) and rest on [Natural Rights](/fundamental-principles/natural-rights.md).
DiLorenzo emphasizes Bastiat's anticipation of rent-seeking and the corruption of education and charity by the state. America came closest to the ideal except for slavery and tariffs.

## Key Points

For the foundational treatment, see [the definition of law](/fundamental-principles/law-as-organization-of-defense.md) and [the proper sphere of law](/true-law/proper-sphere.md).
- Government's only legitimate end is protection of life, liberty, and property. (see Natural Rights).
- Legal plunder includes tariffs, subsidies, progressive taxes, public schooling, welfare. (see legal plunder).

For more, see [legal plunder](/legal-plunder/definition.md).
