# Natural Rights

These principles are developed in [The Law](/the-law.md) and rest on [Natural Rights](/fundamental-principles/natural-rights.md).
These three gifts precede and justify law. Any statute violating them is illegitimate. See how they ground the [definition of law](/fundamental-principles/law-as-organization-of-defense.md).

## Key Points

For the foundational treatment, see [the definition of law](/fundamental-principles/law-as-organization-of-defense.md) and [the proper sphere of law](/true-law/proper-sphere.md).
- Three elements: personality (individuality), liberty, property. (see Natural Rights).
- Legislation must serve them, not create or destroy them. The opposite occurs through the [causes of perversion](/perversion-of-law/causes-of-perversion.md). (see Natural Rights).

For more, see [legal plunder](/legal-plunder/definition.md). Related consequences appear in the discussion of [rent-seeking behavior](/legal-plunder/consequences/rent-seeking.md). Related consequences appear in the discussion of [modern examples](/modern-relevance/contemporary-plunder.md).
