# Law Defined

These principles are developed in [The Law](/the-law.md) and rest on [Natural Rights](/fundamental-principles/natural-rights.md).
This is the central thesis. Law has no other legitimate mission. When it goes beyond defense it becomes plunder. This definition is referenced from almost every other concept in the bundle.

## Key Points

For the foundational treatment, see [the definition of law](/fundamental-principles/law-as-organization-of-defense.md) and [the proper sphere of law](/true-law/proper-sphere.md).
- Collective force may only do what the individual may lawfully do. (see Natural Rights).
- Results in minimal, just, stable government. The opposite occurs through the [causes of perversion](/perversion-of-law/causes-of-perversion.md). (see Natural Rights).

For more, see [legal plunder](/legal-plunder/definition.md).
