# The Law (1850)

These principles are developed in [The Law](/the-law.md) and rest on [Natural Rights](/fundamental-principles/natural-rights.md).
Bastiat shows that life, liberty and property precede legislation. The law (see [/fundamental-principles/law-as-organization-of-defense.md](/fundamental-principles/law-as-organization-of-defense.md)) is the organization of the natural right of defense. When perverted (by greed or false philanthropy) it becomes an engine of legal plunder. The remedy is to return law strictly to its defensive sphere. See also the detailed treatment of the [core definition](/fundamental-principles/law-as-organization-of-defense.md).

## Key Points

For the foundational treatment, see [the definition of law](/fundamental-principles/law-as-organization-of-defense.md) and [the proper sphere of law](/true-law/proper-sphere.md).
- Personality, liberty and property are natural and pre-political. (see Natural Rights).
- Law = collective force organized only for protection. (see Natural Rights).
- Perversion turns law into legalized theft benefiting some at expense of others.
- Consequences: moral confusion, politicized society, rent-seeking, impoverishment.
